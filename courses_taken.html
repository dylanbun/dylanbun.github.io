CSE 440: Introduction to HCI, Sp22
Introduction to the iterative design process in HCI (paper prototyping, design methods, design diamond, storyboarding, heuristics, patterns, inspection, critique). Also covered HCI theory and techniques such as Fitts' law, Sapir-Whorf, affordances, metaphors, and history of design.
The coursework consisted of biweekly checkpoints/indicators of progress that were to be completed as a group. Typically having one person be the "PM"/leader would make this easier to allocate the workload and keep track of progress. Since I took on this role, the weekly time commitment for me was 3 to 5 hours.
CSE 332: Data Structures and Parallelism, Wi22
Focused and implemented abstract data types and structures such as dictionaries, priority queues, graphs, chaining hash tables, and balancing trees. Covered graph theory concepts such as minimum spanning trees, shortest path, topoligcal sort, and searching. Briefly went into concurrency and parallelism specifically with forking.
The coursework consisted of weekly exercises and labs (that would be done with a partner). The exercises would usually be 1-3 hours but the labs would range between 10 to 18 hours per week depending on how effective you could debug.
CSE 312: Foundations of Computing II, Wi22
Introduction to probability and how to model probability using NumPy. Covered counting, combinations, permutations, discrete and continious distributions, MLE, MAP, MOM, bootstrapping, and bandits.
The coursework consisted of a pset every other week which could be completed with a partner. (This may vary from other offerings as Alex Tsun was a guest lecturer). Weekly time commitment ranged from 10 to 15 hours where majority of the hours came from the later psets.
CSE 351: The Hardware/Software Interface, Au21
Introduction to C, various number representation schemes, assembly, memory management, and how high-level machine architecture is implemented.
The coursework consisted of labs every other week (which could be done with a partner) and participation in lecture. Weekly time commitment ranged from 5 to 10 hours depending on how the lab was going.
CSE 311: Foundations of Computing I, Au21
Introduction to logic, set theory, finite state machines, and P vs NP. Also served as an introduction to proofs (similar to discrete math, math 300).
The coursework consisted of weekly assignments of on average six to eight questions with writing proofs. Weekly time commitment ranged from 7 hours to 15 hours as this was my first time working with proofs.
